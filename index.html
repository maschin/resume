Singer

In my earliest days, we played for no money. In future we released several successful albums, including December's Children (And Everybody's), Aftermath and Between the Buttons. I also learned to play guitar and contributed guitar parts for certain songs on Sticky Fingers. Later in the decade we ventured into genres like disco and punk with the album Some Girls. 2011

SuperHeavy

Singer

We wanted a convergence of different musical styles... We were always overlapping styles, but they were nevertheless separate. We began recording our self-titled debut album in early 2009 at a studio in Los Angeles. We recorded about 35 hours of music, with some songs originally lasting "an hour and 10 minutes" before we were whittled down. The album was previewed at Jim Henson Studios, Los Angeles, on 30 June 2011. The band played eight of the recorded songs at the event. Our debut single, "Miracle Worker", released on 6 July 2011 received mostly positive reviews. I sing in Sanskrit on "Satyameva Jayathe" ("truth alone triumphs"), the second single, released on 9 August 2011. Composed by Rahman, the song also features Stewart, Stone and Marley.
